Plastic Propoganda

Plastic is an industry that is prevelant in consumer products. From take away containers to packaging material plastic is everywhere in our lives. However, the idea that the plastic we put into our recycling bins is recycled is largely a lie that has been purpotrated by the producers of plastic in order to make money. As an aside you can see that the 10 cent refund for each container policy in South Australia has seemingly impacted the amount of recycling in SA compared to every other state or territory.

This website seeks to illuminate just how much plastic is really recycled as well as some bonus information on the decomposition time for the different types of plastic.

Description

Our Australia GeoMap is a interactive map which demonstrates through colour saturation, the volume of plastic waste created for each state or territory from 2006 to 2018 (data from 2007, 2011 and 2012 was not included in the government database). A filter menu is included so that the user can drill down to find specific statistics based on the year, plastic type, stream (origin of the waste), and the eventual fate of the waste.
A bar chart is included to help the user visualise the volume between states, which of course updates when the filters are changed. The user can also hover over a specific state or territory to display a separate bar chart that compares the landfill and recycling numbers. Feel free to play around with the filters and hover functionailty whilst keeping in mind you can reset the filters to 'All' by clicking the handing reset button.
With relative ease the user should be able to see the state/territoy's volume of waste and just how much of that ends up in landfill.

Graph 2 is a line chart, with a purpose of demonstrating the wide gap between plastic that ends up in landfill and plastic that is recycled. The line chart a ranges from 2006 to 2018 (data from 2007, 2011 and 2012 was not included in the government database). Energy from waste facility started tracking from 2008, however as you can see the amount of plastic that is converted to energy is negligble compared to what ends up in recycling.

Description

We are all pretty familiar with the plastic recycling logo we see on plastic products. Did you know this is another extension of Plastic Propoganda? You may see the recycling logo on a plastic product but that doesn't mean the plastic product is recyclable, rather it is categorising what type of plastic the product is made from.

  1. Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET)
  2. High density polyethylene (HDPE)
  3. Polyvinyl chloride (PVC)
  4. Low density polyethylene (LDPE)
  5. Polypropylene (PP)
  6. Polystyrene (PS)
  7. Other plastics
Graph 3 seeks to demonstrate the amount of plastic Australia generates according to these "Recycling" symbols broken down by years. By hovering over each category the user is shown the specific tonnage figure of plastic as well as a description of how long this type of plastic takes to decompose. By clicking the 'Run Animation' button the user can watch the graph cycle through the years demonstrating the change in consumption of each type of plastic.
Ultimately, we are hoping the user can get a deeper understanding of just how long plastic takes to decompose, thereby making them think about ones own use of plastic products.
